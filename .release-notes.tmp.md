Fixes the "Bulk AI Fix is slow and invisible to the Job Queue" bug.

## What was wrong

- The Bulk AI Fix page fragmented your selection into batches of 5 posts client-side and fired sequential AJAX requests. Each batch did 10 sequential AI calls on the server.
- For 1,494 posts this meant 299 browser round-trips, each waiting 20–50s on AI calls — effectively hours.
- Because every request carried only 5 posts, it never tripped the ">10 posts → Job Queue" threshold, so the Background Jobs page couldn't see what was happening.

## What changed

- **Bulk AI Fix now sends the full selection in one request.** Server routes 5+ posts to the Job Queue automatically. You'll see the job on the Background Jobs page within seconds.
- **Job Queue processes in parallel batches of 20** with `curl_multi` when Bedrock is the active provider. For 1,494 posts this drops total time from hours to minutes.
- **Live progress bar** on the Bulk AI Fix page polls job status every 2 seconds and shows `processed/total`, percent, and status. A stalled queue auto-triggers a self-tick so you don't have to wait for WordPress cron.
- **Small batches (<5 posts) still run in-request** using the same parallel Bedrock `curl_multi` call for instant results.

## New helper

`ssf_get_job` AJAX endpoint — returns `id, status, total, processed, percent, failed, error` for live UI polling by third-party integrations.
